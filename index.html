Projects - Hardware

Blystwatch

Blystwatch is one of the first PCBs I've designed from scratch and serves as a demo board to showcase development with both the BLYSTL15 (based on the Nordic Semiconductor's nrf54L15) along with the firmware IOComposer AI from I-Syst.inc. The design includes a 6-axis motion LSM6DSV16X, nPM1304 Power Management IC, TMP117 temperature sensor, MAX30102 pulse oximeter and heart-rate sensor, SSD1306 OLED screen and an Ambient Light Sensor bundled in a low-cost 2-layer PCB. It comes along with a dock that seamlessly connects to its exposed copper pads for charging and firmware upload (using a JTag Cortex Debugger). I am currently in the process of ordering the parts for manual assembly and designing its 3D case in Solidworks.

SuperSpeed USB-C Multiplexer (HD3SS3220)

This is a project designed to bridge the gap between our FPGA board and modern USB-C devices, offering a great look into how the devices we interact with daily actually work under the hood! A high-speed PCB bridging a Type-C 3.0 connector with the TI HD3SS3220 MUX to a corresponding Type-A connector. Designing this board required navigating strict impedance controls, signal integrity preservation, and complex power topologies.

I successfully implemented the 5W differential spacing rule, constrained via stubs to < 15mils, and resolved significant DC bias derating challenges by transitioning bulk capacitance to Tantalum Polymer packages.

ANC System at a Distance

At the end of my first term of university, we were given the chance to create our own projects with the purpose of solving a real-world issue that affected a large percentage of patients in hospitals. That problem was Hospital Induced Delirium, a severe state of confusion and cognitive impairment that could cause devastating consequences such as memory loss and hallucinations. From going through various research papers, our team narrowed down one of the major causes of this condition, that being excessive noise levels. By combining a Raspberry Pi as our main computing unit, two microphones to serve as reference and error adjustment, and a speaker, we were able to make a prototype of our solution within the short span of a month. We learned to leverage the MATLAB audio toolbox along with the RPi Support Package to create an automatic calibration system and a continuous noise cancellation loop that works at long distances.

Projects - Software

BlueIO832-Mini App Bundle

The BlueIO832-Mini is a Bluetooth Low Energy microcontroller based on Nordic Semiconductor's nrf52832 SoC. It is aimed to provide an intuitive learning experience similar to the Arduino, but with a lower entry level that doesn't require knowing how to code.

I was fortunate to be able to write the four mobile applications that pair with this microcontroller and serve as the medium to configure the board's pin functions. Each of these four apps allows the user to effortlessly interface with external hardware, configure communication protocols, and monitor sensor data without ever needing to write a single line of embedded firmware.

Mai

Coming soon on both iOS and Google Play, Mai is a mobile application that takes period tracking one step further than other menstrual apps. Along with its light predictive engine, Mai pairs with the Blystpad, a smart bluetooth heatpad, in order to help reduce period cramps. Mai notifies the user about their upcoming menstruation dates and reminds them to use the Blystpad based on increasingly accurate period predictions from past user data. To keep the app lightweight, the predictive engine uses a simple K-NN machine learning algorithm paired with an Exponential Moving Average fallback mechanism that dynamically adjusts based on historical user data deviations.

Hardware Debugging & Constraint Fixes

A breakdown of the critical design constraints and schematic fixes implemented during the routing of the HD3SS3220 MUX and ME6228 LDO.

1. High-Speed Trace Routing Constraints

  • Trace Length Matching: Intrapair lengths matched (TX+ to TX-, RX+ to RX-) using serpentines within 0.25 inches of the IC breakout. TX and RX pairs do not need to match each other.
  • Spacing (5W Rule): Maintained spacing of 5x the trace width between HS signal pairs (e.g., 30 mils for 6 mil traces).
  • Keep-Outs: Kept >30 mils away from generic signals, >50 mils from clocks/oscillators, and >90 mils from the edge of the GND reference plane. Kept 1.5W away from plane voids.
  • Via Discontinuity: Restricted via stubs to <15 mils to prevent resonance. Back-drilling is required for any stubs exceeding this length.

2. Signal Integrity & AC Coupling

  • AC Capacitors: Used 0402 packages symmetrically placed on TX lines ONLY (100nF). Removed redundant RX caps, as the HD3SS3220 utilizes adaptive common-mode voltage tracking (0V - 2V).
  • GND Voiding: Voided the GND reference plane by 60% directly beneath the SMD pads of the AC caps (spanning at least 2 layers deep) to balance capacitive and inductive discontinuities.
  • ESD Protection: Placed flow-through routing diodes immediately at the connector, eliminating sharp corners and tying the GND pin with large vias to handle high-current IEC strikes.

3. Power Topology & Decoupling

  • LDO (ME6228): Used 1µF 0402 ceramic caps. Due to the severe DC bias derating of tiny 0402 packages under load, these were over-spec'd to a 10V/16V rating to ensure they retained true 1µF capacitance. Thermal relief relied on wide copper polygon pours on the GND and VIN/VOUT pins.
  • MUX Decoupling: Added local energy reservoirs (100nF) physically touching the VCC33 and VDD5 pins to combat parasitic inductance during rapid SuperSpeed switching transients.
  • 100µF Bulk Capacitance: Bypassed massive 1210/1812 MLCCs to avoid severe DC bias loss. Replaced with Tantalum Polymer (EIA 1206/1210) or Aluminum Electrolytics, which deliver a true 100µF under a 5V load without derating.

4. Logic & Indicator LED Corrections

  • Wetting Current: Implemented 10kΩ pull-up resistors on ENn_CC and ENn_MUX. This prevents the traces from acting as antennas for 5Gbps EMI noise, while providing just enough "wetting current" (0.5mA on 5V, 0.33mA on 3.3V) to punch through oxidation on mechanical DIP switch contacts without wasting power.
  • VBUS LED Fix: Separated the VBUS indicator from the 900kΩ detection pin. A 900kΩ resistor restricts flow to 3.3µA, starving the LED. Created a parallel path pulling ~3mA through a 1kΩ resistor to ground.
  • DIR Pin LED Fix: The DIR pin is an Open-Drain output (sinks to ground, outputs no voltage). It is rated to sink roughly 1.6mA. Wired the LED directly from the 3.3V rail through a 1kΩ resistor into the DIR pin, allowing the chip to safely sink the current to ground when CC2 is detected.
